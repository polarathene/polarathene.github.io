Thoughts on various problem-solving techniques

          
    
            
  • 
              Pseudocode:
              
    Great way to rough out a plan of attack :+1:
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Trying something:
              
    Very helpful!
    
              
    Figuring out how to do portions of the task by learning and trying how to write smaller functionality, and then integrating it all together :sunglasses:
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Rubber ducky method:
              
    I find this complements the previous two techniques quite well.
    
              
    I often pose some questions, breaking it down and thinking about how to answer those helps quite a bit!
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Reading error messages:
              
    They're magical once you can decipher what they're throwing a fuss about :sweat_smile:
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Using console.log():
              
    Praise be to my Masolow's hammer!
    
              
    May I bathe in the blissful ignorance of not knowing better debugging tools :joy:
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Googling:
              
    Almost always reliable (given sufficient time and effort).
    
              
    A solid first step to external assistance before disturbing the living.
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Asking your peers for help:
              
    One of my last avenues.. and often unfruitful when I've already exhausted previous options.
    
              
    However when it's an area of knowledge new to me that isn't too niche, and especially under the pressure of time, I think reaching out sooner has a higher chance of success.
    
              
    Sometimes I will seek guidance from peers early, while I investigate and learn solo, getting a response is a bonus :)
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Asking coaches for help:
              
    This is kind of the same as peers, just more experienced?
    
              
    When available, I would reach out when I've been stuck and not having luck with anything else thus far.
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Improving your process with reflection:
              
    This is quite helpful too.
    
              
    I try to reflect afterwards (time permitting), and write down notes about anything useful, collect some snippets of code or add links related to the topic.
    
              
    I may not ever need the information again, or it could be years away and become outdated, but when I do, wow, thank you past me! :heart:
